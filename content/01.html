Types of Vinegar Used in Japanese Cooking

A comprehensive guide to different types of vinegar used in Japanese cuisine
By Japanese Cuisine Expert • May 28, 2025 • Cooking

1. Grain Vinegar = 穀物酢 (Kokumotsusu)

This vinegar is made from many kinds of grains such as rice, barley, sake lees, and corn. This is probably the most popular type of vinegar in Japan. It has a strong acidity but is also very refreshing. It can be applied to many dishes since it doesn't have much distinctive aroma.

2. Rice Vinegar = 米酢 (Komezu)

The major component in this vinegar is only rice. It's not as tangy as grain vinegar. The rice gives this vinegar a nice sweetness and also brings a mild tanginess to it. I use this rice vinegar most often since my kids are sensitive to acidity. Also, it goes well with rice such as making sushi rice.

3. Sushi Vinegar = すし酢 (Sushizu)

Sushi vinegar is vinegar seasoned with salt and sugar. Some brands are also flavored with kombu (a type of kelp). This vinegar is not only for sushi rice, but is great for making dressings. My kids are sensitive to acidity, so I prefer to use this one since it's balanced with tanginess and sweetness.

4. Black Vinegar = 黒酢 (Kurozu)

The major component of this vinegar is brown rice and barley. It has a deeper, mellow and more unique flavor than the other vinegars. I barely use this kind since the black color changes the overall look of a dish. I should use this more often since it is more nutritious than the other vinegars!

5. Ponzu = ポン酢 (Ponzu)

This is not 100% pure vinegar. It usually contains vinegar + soy sauce + sugar + citrus juice (yuzu, kabosu etc.) It has acidity like other vinegars, but with more flavor. I use this as a dressing. The taste of ponzu really differs depending on the brand, so try a few to find your preference!
